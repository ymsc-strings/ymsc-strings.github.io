Research Summary

Nicolai Reshetikhin's research links quantum groups, integrable systems, representation theory, and low-dimensional topology. His recent papers revisit this circle of ideas through irregular KZ equations, Kac-Moody representations, Rozansky-Witten foams, quantum integrable systems on classical integrable backgrounds, Calogero-Moser-Sutherland systems, and two-dimensional Yang-Mills theory with corners. The common thread is the construction of algebraic and geometric mechanisms that turn topological or representation-theoretic data into exactly solvable quantum systems, and conversely use integrability to illuminate topology.
