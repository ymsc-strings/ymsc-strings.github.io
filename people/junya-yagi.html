Research Summary

Junya Yagi studies exact correspondences between low-dimensional quantum field theories and integrable models, starting from supersymmetric field theory, topological field theory, and string/M-theory. His earlier work clarified relations among compactifications of 6d SCFTs, the AGT correspondence, and three-dimensional TQFT. In the past five years he has further developed three-dimensional integrable lattice models in M-theory, the tetrahedron equation and its quantum-field-theoretic interpretation, and, with collaborators, interpreted Q-operators as 't Hooft lines, revealing deep links between gauge-theory defect operators and structures in integrable models.
